Bali is the major province in Indonesia.
It is renowned for its highly developed arts, including traditional and modern dance, sculpture, painting, leather, metalworking, and music.
Bali is the home of the Subak irrigation system, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Bali, the “Island of the Gods”,an exotic location because it has a welcoming, kind culture and some of the best beaches, surfing, temples, rice terraces, etc. on Earth.

Mount Batur

one of Bali’s most active volcanoes, a popular challenge for hikers.
On this tour, moonlight climb,
watch the sunrise from the top of the volcano, and then trek around the crater,
enjoying stunning views over the surrounding countryside.

Pura Lempuyang Luhur

The fantastic, high-altitude temple.
As clouds surround you, take some time to explore the temple
in all its glory, meditate, pray, or watch a ceremonial priest-led chant.
This temple consists of seven unique temples.

Ulun Danu Beratan Temple

This Shaivite water temple is one of the easiest temples to visit in Bali
and one of the most impressive.
It’s a much cooler temple to visit
Strolling through the green gardens and exploring the temple feels like entering a serene oasis
